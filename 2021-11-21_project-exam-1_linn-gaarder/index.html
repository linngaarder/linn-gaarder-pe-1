Launch schedule - 2021

Date: November 24, 2021 - 1:21 a.m. Eastern

Mission: Double Asteroid Redirection Test

Description: Double Asteroid Redirection Test (DART), NASA's first flight demonstration for planetary defense, seeks to test and validate a method to protect Earth in case of an asteroid impact threat. The mission aims to shift an asteroid's orbit through kinetic impact – specifically, by impacting a spacecraft into the smaller member of the binary asteroid system Didymos to change its orbital speed.

Date: Dec. 4, 2021, 4:04 – 6:04 a.m. Eastern

Mission: Laser Communications Relay Demonstration (LCRD)

Description: LCRD will fly as a payload on STPSat-6, the primary spacecraft of the third Space Test Program (STP-3) mission for the Department of Defense. STP-3 will lift off on a United Launch Alliance Atlas V 551 rocket from Space Launch Complex 41 at Cape Canaveral Space Force Station in Florida.

Date: December 9, 2021

Mission: Imaging X-Ray Polarimetry Explorer

Description: The x-ray astronomy satellite will study active galactic nuclei, microquasars, pulsars and pulsar wind nebulae, magnetars, accreting X-ray binaries, supernova remnants, and the galactic center.

Date: Late December 2021

Mission: SpaceX Commercial Resupply Mission-24

Description: The 24th flight in the series of NASA's SpaceX cargo resupply missions to the International Space Station. Liftoff of the cargo Dragon spacecraft on a Falcon 9 rocket is targeted for late December from Launch Complex 39A at NASA’s Kennedy Space Center in Florida.
